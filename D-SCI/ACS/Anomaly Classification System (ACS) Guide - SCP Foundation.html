Anomaly Classification System
ACS GUIDE — COMPREHENSIVE REFERENCE DOCUMENT
▶ DOCUMENT RATIFIÉ CLEARANCE NV. 2+ SCP FONDATION — USAGE INTERNE

Vue d'ensemble — Classifications ACS

The Anomaly Classification System (ACS) provides a standardized framework for classifying anomalous objects contained by the Foundation. It extends and replaces the classic single-axis "Object Class" with a multi-axis approach covering Containment, Disruption, and Risk.

Clearance Level
An adaptation of existing Clearance Levels. Refers to the level of an employee granted permission to access the information contained within the document.
Containment Class

Replaces the classic "Object Class." Describes how difficult the anomaly is to contain. This is the Contain axis of "Secure, Contain, Protect."

Disruption Class

New class. Refers to an anomaly's ability to disturb the status quo and/or break the Veil. This is the Secure axis.

Ask: How swiftly does influence spread? How far if unchecked? How easily can the Foundation neutralize its effects?

Risk Class

New class. Refers to the severity of an anomaly's effects on an individual and how easily one can recover. This is the Protect axis.

Ask: How easily can individuals recover? How severe are the effects? At what proximity do effects manifest?

Secondary Class

Optional. Also known as the "Esoteric Class." Uses existing Esoteric Classes. It is standard for Containment Class to be set to "Esoteric" when a Secondary Class is utilized, but this is not required.

Descriptions Détaillées

Clearance Level

Clearance Levels depend largely on the headcanon of the author and the reader. The descriptions below refer to a specific way that the Foundation is run and may not be inline with your view of the Foundation. Use these as a general guideline and adapt as you see fit.

Level 1: Unrestricted (UR)
Available to all Foundation employees. If someone receives a regular paycheck from the Foundation, they can access this document.
Level 2: Restricted (RS)
Available to the majority of employees upon request. If an employee has a subordinate, Level 2 access is already granted. Otherwise, they must request from their supervisor.
Level 3: Confidential (CF)
Available to the minority of employees. Primary administrative staff of a Site will have Level 3 access. Access must be requested through supervisory chains otherwise.
Level 4: Secret (SC)
ONLY available to site and Foundation administrative staff. Access without Level 4 clearance will generally be declined and only approved for very specific purposes.
Level 5: Top Secret (TS)
ONLY available to the highest Foundation Administrative Staff, such as the Ethics Committee or the O5 Council. Anyone without Level 5 access will always be denied.
Level 6: Cosmic Top Secret (CTS)
ONLY available to a member of the O5 Council. All access is granted solely at the discretion of an O5 Council member.

Disruption Class

Disruption Class refers to an anomaly's ability to disturb the status quo and/or break the Veil. When evaluating, consider: how swiftly the influence spreads, how far it will extend if left unchecked, and how easily the Foundation can neutralize its effects.

DARK
VLAM
KENEQ
EKHI
AMIDA
Dark
The known or potential disruption is so low as to not be a concern. The object is essentially inert when not interacted with and may potentially only affect a single individual. The Foundation would find it trivial to clean up after it.
Vlam

The known or potential disruption would be localized to a small handful of people.

The anomalous effects certainly affect multiple people, but would not extend very far. The Foundation would find it relatively simple to neutralize its effects.

Keneq

The known or potential disruption would extend to a number of people roughly defined as a city.

The anomalous effects could spread with fairly significant speed, fast enough as to cause concern. The Foundation would find it moderately difficult to neutralize its effects.

Ekhi

The known disruption would extend to roughly the size of a large metropolitan area to an entire country. The potential disruption could extend to the entire known world.

The spread would be swift and difficult to manage. The Foundation would find it quite difficult to neutralize its effects.

Amida

Reserved for special circumstances when the Foundation is essentially "declaring war" on an anomaly — when the threat poses such a dire risk to the status quo that all possible options must be used to neutralize it.

The effects of an Amida anomaly would extend to the entire known world and possibly the entire universe.

Risk Class

Risk Class refers to the severity of an anomaly's effects on an individual and how easily one can recover from those effects. When evaluating, consider: how easily can individuals recover (if at all), how severe are the effects, and at what proximity do these effects begin to manifest?

NOTICE
CAUTION
WARNING
DANGER
CRITICAL
Notice
The anomalous effects are nearly non-existent to mild. An individual within close proximity would not feel anything as a result of the object. It poses no danger to any individual nearby.
Caution

The anomalous effects are mild to moderate.

An individual within close proximity may feel mild effects from the anomalous object. It poses mild danger to any individual nearby.

Warning

The anomalous effects are moderate to significant.

An individual within close proximity will feel mild effects or may feel major effects from the anomalous object. It poses moderate danger to anyone nearby.

Danger

The anomalous effects are significant to extreme.

An individual within close proximity will feel major effects or may feel extreme effects. It poses significant danger to any individual nearby.

Critical

Reserved for objects whose effects will be near-instant and/or extremely severe. Death is incredibly likely and expected. Any individual nearby will feel the effects near-instantly and the possibility of recovery is impossible.
